Redesigning Taiwan Railway Website

Column 1 Column 2
Project Goal Redesign the website and ticket-booking processto allow for smoother, more user-friendly booking
Project Summary To redesign the interface of the Taiwan Railwaywebsite, I created two different wireframes for twocommonly performed tasks on the website.Next, I picked one wireframe set and createdmockups for three form factors.

*This was an assignment in course Information Design by UCSD on Coursera.

Design Process

Define

I choose to redesign the website based on the procedure of two tasks, booking the round-trip tickets and booking available tickets.

Design

I used Balsamiq to make low-fidelity wireframes that map out my redesign of the website. I designed three key screens for two tasks that I want to redesign based on areas that left room for improvement from previous experience.

  • TASK 1
  • Buy a round-trip ticketfrom Taipei to Taichung / Go to Taichung on Nov 1,and then come back toTaipei on Nov 3.
  • TASK 2
  • Buy the Available Ticketsfor a recent date (ex: tomorrow). 
-Wireframes-

I added in a process bar to indicate the length of the booking procedure, which I felt would allow the users to better envision the general procedure of the task. I also added in a section showing search results to allow users to choose tickets by themselves, which was not an option on the original website.

-Mocups- (laptop, tablet, phone)

I later used Sketch to create mockups for booking round-trip tickets in order to highlight the significance of typography and color in the look and feel of the website.

I designed the mockups around three form factors (phones, tablets and laptops) to get a feel for the best style and layout that works for each screen size.

Learnings

Through this assignment, I learned that it is better to start with a few specific tasks in mind when redesigning the website interfaces. Doing so forces you to view the working process from a user’s perspective, so that you can anticipate how users might react to the system on specific stages of the task, and avoid looking at the interfaces with the eye of a system provider. It also allows you to focus on just the essential information, thus preventing the inclusion of too much unrelated information from the start, which may shift the focus away from designing the main content.

These two points are very different takeaways from those of my earlier experience in planning the “Learning English During Commutes” mobile application, in which we designed the interfaces only from a designer’s perspective.
